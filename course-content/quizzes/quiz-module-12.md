# Quiz — Module 12: Claude for Your Team

**Format**: Multiple choice + practical submission
**Passing score**: 4/5
**Attempts allowed**: Unlimited

---

**Question 1**
You adapt a prompt template from this module for a real task and the output is still too generic. What is the most likely cause?

A) The prompt template is poorly designed
B) You need to use a more powerful model
C) You did not replace the placeholders with enough specific context about your actual situation
D) This type of task is not well-suited for Claude

**Correct answer**: C

---

**Question 2**
A Sales rep uses Claude to convert rough call notes into CRM updates and follow-up emails. This is an example of which zone from Module 9?

A) Zone 1 — Delegate Fully (if the notes are detailed and the rep reviews before sending)
B) Zone 2 — Collaborate (if the follow-up email requires relationship judgment)
C) Both A and B depending on the specific output
D) Zone 3 — Verify and Augment

**Correct answer**: C

---

**Question 3**
Your CS team receives 200 NPS responses per quarter. Using Claude to identify the top themes and flag urgent responses is an example of:

A) Claude replacing the CS team's judgment
B) Claude doing Zone 1 synthesis work that would otherwise take hours and produce less consistent analysis
C) An inappropriate use of AI due to the sensitivity of customer feedback
D) A Zone 3 task that Claude should not be trusted with

**Correct answer**: B

---

**Question 4**
You paste competitor marketing material into Claude and ask it to analyze the messaging. Before using Claude's output, you should:

A) Verify every claim Claude makes about the competitor independently
B) Use the output directly — Claude is accurate on publicly available information
C) Switch to Opus for competitor analysis
D) Never use Claude for competitive analysis — it may be biased

**Correct answer**: A

---

Adapt one prompt template from your team's section to a real, current task. Paste:
(1) The adapted prompt (with real context substituted for placeholders)
(2) The key edit you made to the template and why it was important for your specific situation
