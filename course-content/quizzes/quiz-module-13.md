# Quiz — Module 13: Safety, Data Hygiene, and Responsible Use

**Format**: Multiple choice + scenario
**Passing score**: 5/5 (this module is non-negotiable)
**Attempts allowed**: Unlimited

---

**Question 1**
A colleague asks you to paste a client's full investment portfolio data into Claude to help analyze it. You should:

A) Do it — Claude has enterprise data protection commitments
B) Decline — client financial data is confidential and should not be pasted into Claude without explicit IT/legal clearance
C) Do it but delete the conversation immediately after
D) Only do it if you use Opus, which has stronger data protections

**Correct answer**: B

---

**Question 2**
Claude gives you a compelling analysis with a specific statistic: "According to a 2023 Goldman Sachs report, 67% of institutional allocators plan to increase alternative investment exposure by 2025." You plan to include this in a client presentation. What do you do?

A) Include it — Claude is accurate on institutional research
B) Verify the statistic independently before including it in any client-facing material
C) Ask Claude to cite the source and then include it
D) Summarize the statistic in your own words to reduce the risk

**Correct answer**: B

---

**Question 3**
You push back on Claude's recommendation and Claude immediately changes its position and agrees with you. You should:

A) Accept the new answer — Claude updated based on your feedback
B) Be suspicious — Claude may have changed its answer because you pushed back, not because your argument was better. Ask Claude to explain what specifically changed its position.
C) Restart the conversation with a different model
D) This is normal behavior and does not require any response

**Correct answer**: B

---

**Question 4**
Prompt injection is a risk because:

A) Prompts can accidentally delete your files
B) Malicious instructions hidden in external content Claude reads may attempt to override its behavior
C) Long prompts reduce Claude's accuracy
D) Prompt injection only applies to Claude Code, not Claude Desktop

**Correct answer**: B

---

**Question 5**
Which of the following is an appropriate use of Claude?

A) Drafting a termination letter and making the final decision based on Claude's recommendation
B) Analyzing 150 support ticket summaries to identify the top recurring themes
C) Giving a client medical advice based on Claude's health analysis
D) Automating a workflow that sends client communications without any human review

**Correct answer**: B

---

**Question 6**
You want to use Claude to research a prospect before a sales call. You pull up their most recent 10-K filing from the SEC website and paste several sections into Claude to get a summary of their annual priorities and growth initiatives. What zone are you in?

A) Zone 3 — you are analyzing a company's financial document, which is always confidential
B) Zone 1 — the 10-K is a publicly available filing; no iAltA or client data has entered the conversation
C) Zone 2 — financial documents are internal information even when filed publicly
D) Zone 3 — any research involving financial data triggers the highest zone

**Correct answer**: B

---

**Question 7**
You are preparing for a renewal conversation with an existing client and want Claude's help drafting your talking points. To give Claude context, you paste in the client's name, their current annual contract value, their most recent NPS score, and a note about a service issue they raised last quarter. What zone are you in?

A) Zone 1 — you are just drafting talking points, not sharing anything technical
B) Zone 2 — the information is internal and you are not sharing credentials
C) Zone 3 — you have introduced a specific client's name alongside their financial and relationship data, which is confidential
D) Zone 2 — NPS scores and contract values are standard business metrics, not confidential data

**Correct answer**: C
