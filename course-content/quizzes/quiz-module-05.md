# Quiz — Module 5: Why Specificity is Everything

**Format**: Multiple choice + short answer
**Passing score**: 4/5
**Attempts allowed**: Unlimited

---

**Question 1**
You give Claude a vague prompt and receive a generic, mediocre response. What is the most accurate explanation?

A) Claude was not paying close attention
B) You should use a more powerful model
C) Claude produced the most probable response to a vague input — generic output is the expected result
D) Claude needs to be warmed up with easier questions first

**Correct answer**: C

---

**Question 2**
According to the "new hire" mental model, what is true of every Claude conversation?

A) Claude remembers your preferences from previous sessions
B) Claude is like a new employee on day one — it knows nothing about your context unless you provide it
C) Claude improves over time as it learns your style
D) Claude already knows your industry and company from its training

**Correct answer**: B

---

**Question 3**
Which of the following is NOT one of the three questions every prompt must answer?

A) What do I want?
B) Who is it for?
C) How quickly do I need this?
D) What does good look like?

**Correct answer**: C

---

**Question 4**
You tell Claude: "Keep this under 100 words, use direct language, and do not include any statistics unless I provided them." These are:

A) Restrictions that will limit Claude's output quality
B) Constraints that focus Claude and improve output quality
C) Unnecessary details that Claude will ignore
D) Instructions only relevant for Opus, not Sonnet

**Correct answer**: B
