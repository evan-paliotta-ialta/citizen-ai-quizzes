# Quiz — Module 7: Iteration, Examples, and Getting to Great

**Format**: Multiple choice + short answer
**Passing score**: 4/5
**Attempts allowed**: Unlimited

---

**Question 1**
Claude gives you a first response to a complex task. What is the right mindset?

A) Accept it — Claude's first response is typically its best
B) Reject it and try a different model
C) Treat it as a draft and identify specifically what would make it better
D) Repeat the same prompt to get a different result

**Correct answer**: C

---

**Question 2**
Which follow-up prompt will produce the most useful revision?

A) "Make this better"
B) "Try again"
C) "The opening is too generic — rewrite it to lead with the specific problem this client is facing"
D) "This is not quite right"

**Correct answer**: C

---

**Question 3**
Why are examples so powerful as inputs to Claude?

A) They reduce the number of tokens needed
B) They allow Claude to infer tone, structure, level of detail, and style — not just the words
C) They bypass Claude's safety filters
D) They are only useful for creative writing tasks

**Correct answer**: B

---

**Question 4**
You ask Claude: "What are the weaknesses in what you just wrote?" This technique is useful because:

A) It forces Claude to start over from scratch
B) Claude will often identify problems in its own output — hedging, buried insights, missing sections
C) It resets the context window
D) It switches Claude to a more careful model

**Correct answer**: B

---

**Question 5 — Short Answer**
You have received a Claude output that is technically correct but feels too generic for your audience. Write the specific follow-up prompt you would use to fix it. Be concrete about what is wrong and what you want changed.

*(No single correct answer — graded on specificity and applicability.)*
