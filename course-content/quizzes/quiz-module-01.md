# Quiz — Module 1: What Claude Is (and Isn't)

**Format**: Multiple choice + short answer
**Passing score**: 4/5
**Attempts allowed**: Unlimited

---

**Question 1**
Claude is best described as:

A) A search engine that retrieves facts from the internet
B) A language model that predicts the most probable, coherent response
C) A database that stores and retrieves company information
D) A live assistant that looks up answers in real time

**Correct answer**: B

---

**Question 2**
You ask Claude to analyze a complex strategic question and the response feels shallow and generic. What is the most likely cause?

A) Claude is not capable of complex analysis
B) The question was too long
C) Claude defaulted to a fast, probable answer rather than a thorough one
D) Claude ran out of context window

**Correct answer**: C

---

**Question 3**
Claude gives you a specific statistic to include in a client report. What should you do?

A) Use it — Claude is very accurate with numbers
B) Verify it independently before including it in any document
C) Ask Claude to confirm the statistic a second time
D) Only use it if Claude cited a source

**Correct answer**: B

---

**Question 4**
You start a new Claude conversation on Monday. Claude:

A) Remembers everything from your Friday session
B) Has access to your previous conversations through your account
C) Starts completely blank with no memory of previous sessions
D) Remembers your preferences but not the content of past conversations

**Correct answer**: C

---

**Question 5 — Short Answer**
In your own words, describe one situation in your daily work where you would tell Claude to "take your time" before answering, and why.

*(No wrong answer — this question checks that you can apply the concept to your role.)*
