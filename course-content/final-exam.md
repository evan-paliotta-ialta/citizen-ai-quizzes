## Section 1: Foundations (Questions 1–8)

**Question 1**
Claude is best described as:

A) A search engine that retrieves verified facts
B) A language model that predicts the most probable, coherent response based on training patterns
C) A live assistant with access to real-time information
D) A database of human knowledge that can be queried

**Correct**: B

---

**Question 2**
Claude states confidently that a specific regulation changed in October 2025. You should:

A) Trust it — Claude is accurate on regulatory topics
B) Verify it independently — this may be after Claude's knowledge cutoff or a hallucinated claim
C) Ask Claude to cite its source before using the information
D) Only trust it if you used Opus rather than Sonnet

**Correct**: B

---

**Question 3**
You are about to ask Claude to analyze a complex strategic question. Which prompt opening will produce the most thorough response?

A) "Analyze this for me:"
B) "Quickly, what do you think about..."
C) "Before you answer, take your time and think through this from multiple angles. Consider the strongest arguments on each side before giving me your recommendation."
D) "You are Claude. Analyze this:"

**Correct**: C

**Question 4**
Which of the following should NEVER be entered into Claude Desktop?

A) A publicly available industry report
B) A client's name paired with their investment portfolio details
C) A draft marketing email for review

## Section 2: Prompting and Working With Claude (Questions 9–16)

**Question 9**
Which of the following is the strongest prompt?

A) "Write an email to a prospect."
B) "Write a professional email."
C) "You are a senior sales executive at a B2B data platform. Write a follow-up email to a hedge fund COO we met at a conference yesterday. Reference their comment about data coverage gaps. Under 100 words. Direct tone. End with a soft ask for a 20-minute call next week. Include subject line."
D) "Write an email for sales purposes about our platform."

**Correct**: C

---

**Question 10**
What are the five elements of a well-structured prompt?

A) Question, Answer, Length, Model, Temperature
B) Role, Task, Context, Constraints, Output Format
C) Who, What, When, Where, Why
D) Input, Process, Output, Review, Iterate

**Correct**: B

---

**Question 11**
Claude's first response to a complex task is good but not quite right. What should you do?

A) Start over with a completely new prompt
B) Use a stronger model
C) Identify specifically what is wrong and give a targeted follow-up prompt addressing that issue
D) Accept it — the first response is typically the best

**Correct**: C

**Question 12**
You want Claude to write in your company's specific voice and style, and you want this applied automatically to every conversation. The best approach is:

A) Paste your style guide into every conversation
B) Add your style guide to your Project instructions so it applies automatically
C) Include it in the task description each time you ask for written content
D) Upload it as a document at the start of each conversation

**Correct**: B

**Question 13**
You show Claude an example of an email you were really happy with and ask it to write a new email in the same style. This technique is called:

A) Template prompting
B) Few-shot prompting (showing Claude examples of what good looks like)
C) System prompting
D) Iterative prompting

**Correct**: B

---

**Question 14**
Which Zone does the following task fall into: "Review and finalize the termination decision for an underperforming employee."

A) Zone 1 — Delegate Fully
B) Zone 2 — Collaborate (Claude drafts, you finalize)
C) Zone 3 — Verify and Augment, with the final decision made by a human

**Question 22**
Your manager asks you to use Claude to draft a proposal for a new client engagement worth $2M ARR. The final proposal will be sent to the client. Which approach is most appropriate?
