## Marketing

**Campaign Brief Generation**

*"You are a senior marketing strategist for an alternative investment data platform. I need a campaign brief for [campaign name]. Target audience: [describe]. Core message: [describe]. Channel: [LinkedIn/email/paid/etc.]. Campaign goal: [awareness/leads/engagement]. Tone: direct, data-driven, not salesy. Output the brief with sections for: objective, audience, key messages (3), call to action, and success metrics."*

**Content Repurposing**

*"I have a blog post about [topic]. Repurpose it into: (1) a LinkedIn post under 150 words with a hook, (2) a 3-email nurture sequence for prospects who attended our last webinar, (3) three tweet-length versions testing different angles. Maintain the core argument but adapt the format and length for each channel."*

**Competitor Analysis**

Paste a competitor's website copy, LinkedIn post, or marketing material into Claude:
*"Analyze the messaging approach in the content I pasted. What positioning are they leading with? What pain points are they targeting? What is missing from their narrative that we could own? What would differentiate our message?"*

**Ad Copy Variants**

*"Write 5 versions of a LinkedIn ad for [product/feature]. Each under 50 words. Test these angles: (1) pain point, (2) outcome/result, (3) social proof, (4) curiosity/question, (5) direct offer. Include a headline for each."*

**Brand Voice Check**

Upload your brand style guide to your Project, then:
*"Review the following copy against our brand guidelines. Flag anything that conflicts with our voice, tone, or style rules. Suggest specific rewrites for the flagged sections."*

## Sales

**Pre-Call Research Brief**

*"I have a call with [Name], [Title] at [Company] in 30 minutes. Here is what I know about them: [paste LinkedIn profile, recent news, company description, any prior notes]. Generate a call prep brief with: (1) three things to know about their likely priorities, (2) two relevant talking points connecting their situation to our platform, (3) the most likely objection and how to handle it, (4) a suggested opening question to start the call well."*

**Personalized Outreach**

*"Write a cold outreach email to [Name], [Title] at [Company]. They are likely responsible for [function]. Our platform helps [their type of firm] with [specific problem]. Reference their recent [news/funding/hire/etc.]: [paste relevant info]. Under 100 words. Direct. No filler. End with a specific ask for a 15-minute call. Subject line included."*

**Objection Handling Prep**

*"Generate the 10 most common objections a [Hedge Fund COO / LP / etc.] would raise when evaluating our platform. For each objection, give me: the underlying concern behind the objection, a concise response (under 3 sentences), and a follow-up question to keep the conversation moving."*

**Call Debrief to CRM Note**

*"Here are my rough notes from a sales call today: [paste notes]. Convert these into: (1) a structured CRM deal update (deal stage, key facts, next steps, decision timeline, stakeholders), (2) a follow-up email to the prospect summarizing what we discussed and confirming next steps, (3) one internal Slack message flagging anything I need help with from the team."*

**RFP Response**

*"I am responding to the following RFP question: [paste question]. Here are the relevant facts about our platform: [paste relevant context]. Write a response that is direct, specific, and under 200 words. Avoid marketing language. Lead with the answer, then provide supporting detail."*

## Customer Success

**QBR Preparation**

*"I am preparing for a QBR with [Client Name]. Here is a summary of their account: [paste usage data, support history, key contacts, contract details]. Generate: (1) a 3-point account health summary, (2) three wins to highlight, (3) two areas where we need to address gaps proactively, (4) a recommended agenda for a 45-minute QBR call, (5) two strategic questions to ask to deepen the relationship."*

**Escalation Email**

*"I need to write an escalation response email to [Client Name] about [issue]. Background: [describe the issue, what happened, what we have done so far]. Write an email that: acknowledges the impact honestly, summarizes what we know, explains what we are doing and by when, and closes with a clear commitment. Tone: direct and accountable, not defensive. No corporate non-apology language."*

**Feedback Synthesis**

*"Here are [N] NPS responses / support tickets / meeting notes from clients: [paste]. Identify the top 5 themes. For each theme: give it a name, quote two representative examples, estimate what percentage of responses reflect this theme, and rate the urgency (high/medium/low). Output as a table."*

**Renewal Risk Analysis**

*"Here is the account data for [Client Name]: [paste usage trends, NPS scores, support tickets, contract value, renewal date]. Based on this, assess: (1) the likelihood of renewal on a scale of 1–5 with explanation, (2) the top two risk factors, (3) three specific actions I should take in the next 30 days to improve renewal probability."*

**Knowledge Base Article**

*"I just resolved a support ticket where a client could not [describe issue]. The solution was [describe]. Write a knowledge base article about this issue with sections for: overview, common causes, step-by-step resolution, and when to escalate to support. Keep it under 400 words. Assume the reader is a non-technical end user."*

## HR / People

**Job Description**

*"Write a job description for a [Title] role at iAltA. Level: [senior/mid/junior]. Key responsibilities: [list 4–6]. Required skills: [list]. Nice to have: [list]. Company tone: professional, direct, and unpretentious — we do not use buzzwords. Format with sections: About the Role, What You Will Do, What We Are Looking For, What We Offer. Under 450 words total."*

**Interview Questions**

*"Generate 10 interview questions for a [Title] role. Mix: (2) behavioral questions testing [specific competency], (2) situational questions testing [specific scenario], (2) technical/functional questions, (2) culture and values questions, (2) role-specific questions about [key challenge for this role]. For each question, note what a strong answer would demonstrate."*

**Pulse Survey Synthesis**

*"Here are [N] open-text responses from our quarterly employee pulse survey. Question asked: [paste question]. Identify the top 5 themes. For each: name the theme, quote two representative examples, estimate frequency, and rate the sentiment (positive/neutral/negative). Flag any responses that warrant urgent management attention. Output as a table."*

**Policy Drafting**

*"Draft a [policy name] policy for a company of [N] employees in [industry]. The policy should cover: [list key areas]. Tone: clear and direct — written for employees, not lawyers. Include: purpose, scope, policy details, responsibilities, and exceptions process. Flag any section where we should get legal review before finalizing."*

## Operations / Finance

**Meeting Notes to Action Items**

*"Here are my rough notes from today's [meeting type] meeting: [paste notes]. Convert these into: (1) a clean summary of decisions made, (2) a numbered action item list with owner and deadline for each item, (3) a list of open questions that still need answers. Format clearly with headers."*

**Contract Review**

*"I am uploading a vendor contract. Please: (1) summarize the key obligations on both sides in plain English, (2) flag any clause that is unusual, one-sided, or that I should ask a lawyer to review, (3) extract all dates and deadlines into a table with columns for: date, event, party responsible."*

**Report Summarization**

*"I am uploading a [report type]. Summarize it for a busy executive who has 3 minutes to read. Lead with the two or three most important findings. Flag any numbers or claims I should verify before sharing this summary. Under 200 words."*

**Process Documentation**

*"I need to document our [process name] process. Here is how it works: [describe informally, in rough notes]. Convert this into a clean process document with: overview, step-by-step instructions (numbered), who is responsible for each step, tools used, and common issues and how to resolve them. Format for a new employee reading this for the first time."*

---

## Key Takeaways

1. Every team function has high-leverage Claude use cases — the prompts above are starting points, not final versions
2. Customize every prompt template with your specific context — generic prompts produce generic output
3. The best prompts for your role will come from your own experience — save what works to the team library
4. Cross-functional awareness helps you spot AI-assisted handoffs between teams (e.g., sales passes call notes to CS, CS synthesizes feedback for product)
